The Greatest Meeting This is the saga of the impassioned journeys of two men, their true premonitions of a mystical meeting, and their self-discoveries. Full of compelling adventure, love and betrayal, joy and sadness, their stories have never before been told. This extraordinary true historical novel takes place in Central Asia and the Middle East. It is an unforgettable detailed narrative account of the lives of an enigmatic giant and legendary mystic figure, Shams-e Tabrizi, and the most renowned religious scholar, Molana Jalaleddin Mohammad-e Rumi.

The Howling Leopard is an enchanting tale of a seven-year-old boy, a German shepard, and a monstrously huge servant. The servant, a man with a flamboyant imagination, weaves fantastic war stories full of heroic and daring deeds in which he always plays a leading role. The young boy is captivated by the stories and the romantic figure the servant portrays, he worships and idolizes the man only to later find out he has been deceived. Upon this realization, a 'war' breaks out between the two and is maintained throughout the remainder of their relationship. It is a touching tale of love and betrayal, full of daring exploits, childhood pranks and the sweet and bitter pain of growing up.

Dreams of a Native Son He comes to the United States for an education, planning to return to his native land to use his hard-won knowledge for the benefit of his people. Lost in this society after the completion of his studies, he is tortured and torn apart by his appetite for the good life which he finds so elusive. Floundering, he tries to hide his profound misery in a physical relationship with a lonely woman. A series of dramatic and tragic events along with the influence of an old Indian woman, an African friend, and an aged famous author alters his path of self-destruction. He must face the agonizing decision to leave the shattered pieces of his life and his missing son behind or continue his homeless existence in the vain hope of finding his roots.

The Sunset Drifters is a delightful voyage of self-discovery and transformation of four young foreign students Marxists. Set in California during the 1960s, it narrates the hilarious saga of "the four musketeers", Cock-Eyed Jack, Cool-Hand Jeff, Fred the Magnificent and their sole follower, tirelessly struggles to employ the disenchanted minorities, Blacks, American-Indians, Mexican-Americans, for the ultimate Proletariat Revolution in America.

Bibi's Rainbow is a delightfully narrated novel centered on a large Iranian immigrant family and their old, wise, and faithful nanny, Bibi, a talented cook armed with inexhaustible secret recipes, who is resolutely determined to ease the transition of four generations of her "family" into American society. Using her wits and miraculous recipes as weapons, she ultimately manages to avert a "Clash of Civilizations" in their Beverly Hills neighborhood, winning over the hearts and souls of her extended family and their neighbors during the tumultuous period between 1979-2008. It is a tale of the long, sometimes tragic, often hilarious, journey to assimilation.

Escape From Paradise The lives of a group of Iranians and a young American woman with her two children are seriously threatened in the devastating aftermath of the bloody Iran revolution of 1979. Combined, their lives make a tapestry that reflects the entire colorful canvas of society's different political persuasions and social classes. For reasons of their own, each is blacklisted and cannot leave the country through the normal means. Unbeknownst to each other, they individually employ the services of an underground smuggling ring, run by a profiteer of the revolution, to guide them across the Iran-Turkish border.

The political theory that inspired this book is one that espoused and mixes candor and hope, realism and idealism, in a message to all friends of liberty wherever they ply their honorable trade. The immodest hope of the author of this book is for the message of Children of a Lesser Nation to read: no happiness without liberty, no liberty without self-government, no self-government without a constitution, no constitution without morality―and none of these noble aspirations without stability and order in our world. Ultimately, our future isn’t in the stars, but in our mind and hearts.
